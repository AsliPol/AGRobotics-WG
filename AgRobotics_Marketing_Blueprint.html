Chapter 02

Email Campaign —
Member & Sponsor Outreach

A Mailchimp-ready campaign strategy for re-engaging current members, updating sponsors, and driving Student Competition registrations.

Campaign Strategy

Audience Segmentation

3 distinct lists — 3 distinct messages

Sending one email to everyone wastes reach. Members, sponsors, and student prospects each need different content, tone, and CTAs.

Working Group Members Industry Sponsors Student Prospects
Subject Line Strategy

Test 2 subject lines per send (A/B)

HubSpot training taught me A/B testing on subject lines drives 20–30% open rate improvement. I'd test curiosity vs. direct benefit every campaign.

A: "What happened in Bradford last week?" B: "AgRobotics Q1 2026 — demo results inside"
Send Cadence

Monthly digest + event-triggered sends

Monthly newsletter keeps baseline engagement. Triggered sends (post-demo, competition launch, annual report release) hit when interest is highest.

KPIs I'd track

Open rate · CTR · Unsubscribe · Conversion

Every campaign generates a one-page Google Data Studio summary — shared at the next Working Group meeting.

First-edition events have no playbook. I'd build one from day one — so year two takes half the effort. Here's the starter kit.

My coordination philosophy.

01
Document everything — Year 2 should be effortless

First-edition events are hard. I build templates, run sheets, and decision logs as I go, so the next coordinator inherits a complete playbook, not a blank page.

02
Every event = 5 pieces of content

Pre-event promo, live day posts, recap, data highlight, and a member/student quote card. One event should fill an entire content week.

03
Stakeholders hear from me before they have to ask

Judges get briefed. Students get reminded. Sponsors see their logo in the recap. Proactive communication is the difference between a smooth event and a stressful one.

04
I come from this world — I know why it matters

Growing up in an agriculture family in Antalya means I understand what's at stake when a robot fails a trial, or when a farmer takes a risk on new technology. That context makes my communications authentic.
